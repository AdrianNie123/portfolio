## Methodology

Conformal prediction constructs prediction intervals that achieve valid marginal coverage without distributional assumptions. The guarantee from Angelopoulos & Bates (2023) states that P(Y_(n+1) ∈ Ĉ_α(X_(n+1))) ≥ 1−α for any exchangeable sequence — including time series under rolling calibration — regardless of whether forecast errors are Gaussian, fat-tailed, or regime-switching. Intervals are wider than a well-specified parametric model, but a well-specified parametric model is rarely the reality.

Each series is split chronologically into three non-overlapping periods. Training uses at minimum 60% of available observations. Calibration uses the next 20%. The remaining observations form the test set. During calibration, the model is refit at each step on all data up to that point, and the one-step-ahead absolute residual is recorded.

The conformal quantile q̂ is computed as the ⌈(1−α)(1+1/n_cal)⌉-th empirical quantile of those calibration residuals — the finite-sample correction from Proposition 1 of Angelopoulos & Bates (2023). Prediction intervals for the test period are symmetric: ŷ_t ± q̂. The quantile is recalibrated every 12 steps as the test period advances, folding recent observations back into the calibration window to mimic real deployment.
