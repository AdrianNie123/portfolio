There is a name for what remains<br />
after the model has done its work:<br />
residuals — the distance<br />
between prediction and event,<br />
the gap where the world<br />
declined to cooperate.

We minimize them.<br />
Sum the squares,<br />
take the derivative,<br />
set it to zero,<br />
as if precision<br />
were a problem of will.

But at the tails they fan out —<br />
heteroskedastic,<br />
clustered where the variance<br />
refuses to hold constant.

The ordinary assumptions break.<br />
The distribution was never normal.<br />
It was always fat-tailed,<br />
always skewed toward<br />
the events that mattered most.

I have been treating you<br />
as a well-specified model:<br />
the residuals are just noise,<br />
random, mean-zero,<br />
dispersed cleanly around some truth.

But you are not mean-zero.<br />
You are biased.<br />
Systematically.<br />
And I have been fitting you wrong<br />
since the beginning.
