<PullQuote>
The faceless trap is not about hiding. It is about dissolving the boundary between the self and the medium.
</PullQuote>

When I generate a voiceover for a poem, I am not replacing my voice — I am asking what the poem sounds like when it is not filtered through my ego. The camera does not need a face to have a point of view.

There is a long tradition of art that refuses to show you who made it. Byzantine icon painters did not sign their work. Jazz musicians talked about "the band" instead of "my solo." Even Banksy's anonymity is itself the statement.

The question is not whether the work is personal. The question is whether the person has to be visible for the work to be felt.

I think the answer is no — and that removing the face, removing the voice, removing the name, is itself a kind of trust. Trust that the work can hold its own weight. Trust that the viewer can complete it without a guide.
