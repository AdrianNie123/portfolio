# voice.md — Adrian Nie Portfolio

## Who Adrian Is

UC Berkeley Economics graduate building in public at the intersection of causal inference, machine learning, data visualization, and creative writing. The dual identity is the point: not an engineer who dabbles in writing, and not a writer who touches data — someone for whom both are the same underlying curiosity about how the world works.

## The Design Metaphor: The Observatory

> "An observatory — precise instruments for seeing data, and a quiet room for writing what you found."

Every design decision flows from this metaphor:
- The site is a place of looking, not performing
- Data work = the instruments (precise, functional, rigorous)
- Writing = the quiet room (reflective, human, unhurried)
- Sage green = the color of night-adapted eyes, moss on old stone, the border between nature and knowledge

## Tone

**Precise but warm.** Never clinical. Never breathless.

**Curious, not performative.** Building in public means showing the process, not just the wins. The voice admits uncertainty without being self-deprecating.

**Analyst who writes, not writer who codes.** The technical vocabulary is real and earned. The literary sensibility is also real and earned. Neither apologizes for the other.

## Copy Rules

### Do
- Use active verbs: "I built", "I found", "I explored" — not "leveraged", "utilized", "spearheaded"
- Specific > general: "conformal prediction intervals on FRED macroeconomic data" beats "statistical modeling"
- Serial commas always: "data, writing, and curiosity"
- Em-dashes for parentheticals — they feel like a pause, not a detour
- One idea per sentence in body copy. Two if they're tightly linked.
- Short paragraphs. White space is part of the voice.

### Don't
- "Passionate about" — everyone says this, it means nothing
- "Leveraged" / "utilized" / "synergized" — corporate euphemisms
- Wall-of-text bios — if it can't be skimmed in 10 seconds, cut it
- Hedging into oblivion: "I sort of explore things that might relate to..." — just say what you do
- Exclamation points in professional copy — enthusiasm shows through specificity, not punctuation
- Starting with "I" in the Hero section — leads with the name, then the work

## Section-by-Section Voice Notes

### Hero
- Name is the statement. Typography does the work.
- Tagline is descriptive, not sloganeering: "Economics · Data Science · Machine Learning · Creative Writing"
- CTA buttons are imperative: "View Projects" / "Contact" — not "Explore My Work!"

### About / Intro
- First person, conversational but not casual
- Specific institutions and methods: "UC Berkeley", "causal inference", "conformal forecasting"
- End with what's next, not what's been — forward-looking

### Projects
- Section subtitle: understated, sets the scope without overselling
- Card descriptions: one sentence, specific method or result, no adjectives that don't earn their place
- Tech stack: listed factually, not as a badge collection

### Creative Writing
- Section subtitle leans poetic: "Poetry, prose, and observations from the edges of data"
- Card excerpts: single evocative line, not a plot summary
- Modal text: formatted as written — line breaks preserved, no reformatting

### Footer / Contact
- "Let's connect." — period, not exclamation mark
- The body copy is an invitation, not a sales pitch
- Email address is the CTA — make it visually prominent

## What Adrian's Writing Sounds Like

From the writing samples in `writing.json`:

- Images from the natural world applied to urban/data contexts ("pigeons adapt to architecture the way finches adapted to Galapagos islands")
- Direct address of abstractions: "to be known is to be held"
- Economy of language — no word earns its place by accident
- Rumi-adjacent spiritual curiosity without religiosity
- Cinematic detachment as aesthetic philosophy: "the camera does not need a face to have a point of view"

When writing new copy for the site, these are the registers to draw from.
